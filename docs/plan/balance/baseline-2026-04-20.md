## Hypothesis — why draws dominate

- **Finisher scarcity.** `bulwark-control` (the SimpleSweep deck) has
  no reliable lethal plan against another control-leaning shell. When
  both seats play bulwark-control, integrity attrition alone never
  reaches zero within 6000 input tokens. Eight of 21 SimpleSweep
  matchups drew ≥ 95 % of games; three drew 100 %.
- **Threat-avoidance over aggression.** The `fixed` and `fortress`
  profiles both lean defensive. When paired against a defensive
  counterpart on a defensive deck, neither side seeks Clash — the
  game simply runs out of Aether-efficient attacks to mount.
- **AI floor, not ceiling.** The TiThWave / BulFort pairs *do* win
  when handed an offensive-leaning matchup, so decks aren't pure
  stall; the bots just fail to convert pressure into a finisher
  when paired against peers of similar skill. This matches the plan's
  §"AI floor is bad" exit condition.

Concrete consequence: Step 12.1 should focus on **closing-pressure
weights** (intent `lethal_check`, consideration `conduit_softness`)
and/or **aggression dials** in the experimental profiles. Deck-level
rebalancing is premature until a decisive-rate above 40 % is reachable
on the existing kits.

in draw rate or a shift in the weakest-performer slot is the whole
point of the diff. Do **not** overwrite these two files under the
