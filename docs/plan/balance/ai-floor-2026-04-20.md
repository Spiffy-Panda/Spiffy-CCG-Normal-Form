# Step 12.1 — AI floor fix (2026-04-20)

Outcome: floor rule enforced on all four experimental bots, but
neither edited bot cleared the 55% decisive-game promotion bar in its
bench. Diagnosis: the remaining high draw rate is a card / engine
issue, not a weight issue. **Routing the step-12 arc forward to
Step 12.3 (card threat audit) with a Step 12.2 side-check.**

### Fortress — `bulwark-control`

| Pair     | W | L | D  | Win rate | Avg steps |
|----------|--:|--:|---:|---------:|----------:|
| FortNew  | 2 | 2 | 36 | 5.0 %    | 387.1     |

Decisive games: 4 / 40. Decisive WR for FortNew: **50 %**. Below the

regression — bulwark-control mirrors draw 90 % of the time in either

[`ai-testing-data/12.1-fortress.results.json`](../../../ai-testing-data/12.1-fortress.results.json).

### Reaper — `hollow-disruption`

| Pair    | W  | L  | D  | Win rate | Avg steps |
|---------|---:|---:|---:|---------:|----------:|
| ReapNew | 4  | 11 | 25 | 10.0 %   | 303.0     |
| ReapOld | 11 | 4  | 25 | 27.5 %   | 303.0     |

Decisive games: 15 / 40. Decisive WR for ReapNew: **26.7 %**. A
clear regression. Reaper's pre-edit "no closer" behaviour was
accidentally winning mirrors by letting opponents misplay into
overcommit; forcing it to close pulls it out of that accidental
equilibrium. Avg game length also dropped from 331 (12.0 baseline
HolReap row) to 303 steps — evidence the edit *does* fire, just not
favorably in mirror. Artifact:
[`ai-testing-data/12.1-reaper.results.json`](../../../ai-testing-data/12.1-reaper.results.json).

## PairCorrectly re-run at baseline seed

Exit criterion wants `draw rate ≤ 40 %` on the baseline config after
the floor fix. Re-ran
[`ai-testing-data/PairCorrectly.tournament.json`](../../../ai-testing-data/PairCorrectly.tournament.json)
with the edited bots.

| Metric          | 12.0 baseline | 12.1 re-run |
|-----------------|--------------:|------------:|
| Total games     | 240           | 240         |
| Total draws     | 196           | 196         |
| **Draw rate**   | **81.7 %**    | **81.7 %**  |
| Avg game length | 374.2 steps   | 374.1 steps |

Per-pair numbers are byte-identical to the baseline: `pushing` is
rarely the active intent in matched-pair cross-matchups, so the edit
does not propagate to ecosystem-level outcomes. Artifact:
[`ai-testing-data/12.1-PairCorrectly.results.json`](../../../ai-testing-data/12.1-PairCorrectly.results.json).

**Draw rate ≤ 40 % target missed by 41.7 pp.** Per the 12.1 plan's
"If that target misses, the diagnosis shifts to 12.2 or 12.3 — log
which and proceed" clause:

- **Primary shift: Step 12.3 (card threat audit).** HolReap's bench
  shows the bots *can* play more decisively when the intent fires —
  but `hollow-disruption` still can't convert `pushing` into Conduit
  collapse. That is a closer-density problem at the card pool level.
  Same verdict for `bulwark-control`: Fortress already has the weights
  to close, it just has nothing in-deck to close with. Reaper's
  `notes.md` already calls out the "No closer" weakness explicitly;
  this step confirms that the weight table alone can't fix it.
- **Secondary shift: Step 12.2 (engine sanity pass).** Minimum-turns-
  to-lethal analysis on the most-damage-dense pile the engine
  currently allows. If the ceiling is > 8 turns, card edits alone
  won't reach the ≤ 40 % draw target either.
