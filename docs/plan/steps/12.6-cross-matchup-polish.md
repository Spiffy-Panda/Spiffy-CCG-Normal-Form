## Step 12.6 — Cross-matchup polish

> **Status: blocked on 12.5 (2026-04-20).** The current matchup
> matrix has three polar cells (BulFort-EmbHell 37 draws,
> EmbHell-HolReap 30 draws, BulFort-HolReap 22 draws) and one
> runaway (TiThWave 95.8 %). 12.5 has to settle wavebreaker +
> fortress first so this step's tech-slot edits land against a
> stable matched-pair set. See
> [`12.INDEX-2026-04-20.md`](12.INDEX-2026-04-20.md) for the live
> state of the arc.

The final shape of a healthy card game is archetype
rock-paper-scissors: every archetype beats someone and loses to
someone, and no single pair posts 90%+ win rate against the field.
After 12.5 locks in matched (deck, AI) pairs, this step reshapes
the **matrix** across different-deck matchups until the polar
cells are gone.

Read first:
- The tournament **matchup matrix** (the coloured grid on
  `#/tournament`) from the most recent 12.5 bench run.
- [10.2-long-term-ai-plan.md](10.2-long-term-ai-plan.md) §"Decks × AI"
  — cross-pair matchups are what Option B's affinity declarations
  predict but don't by themselves achieve.

## Scope — in

- **Polarity scan.** From the matchup matrix, list every
  cross-pair cell outside `[25%, 75%]` decisive-game win rate.
  These are the polar matchups that need attention.
- **Tech-slot fixes (cards, not weights).** For each polar pair
  where archetype X routinely loses to archetype Y, add one or two
  tech cards to archetype X's deck that are specifically good in
  the X-vs-Y matchup (e.g. a cheap removal against aggro, an
  anti-reanimation effect against Hollow). Tech slots come out of
  filler slots, never out of closers.
- **Re-verify 12.5.** Adding tech almost always shifts the matched
  AI's scoring environment. Replay the 12.5 procedure on every
  affected bot. If matched-AI dominance drops below the 15 pp bar,
  the weight tune was overfit to the pre-tech card pool — redo.
- **Matrix lock.** Once every cross-pair cell is inside
  `[25%, 75%]`, commit the final state of every deck and
  `stable/` weight set as the closing artifact of the step-12 arc.

## Scope — out

- New engine rules / keywords. If a tech-slot idea needs a new
  mechanic, it's out of scope — park it in the 12.2 engine-audit
  note and defer.
- New decks or new archetypes. The four reference decks are the
  vocabulary.
- AI tuning that isn't direct consequence of a card change in this
  step. The iteration point is deliberate: card first, then
  weights, never the other way.

## Iteration note

This is the only step in the arc where the card → weights loop may
run more than once. Polar matchups can cascade: fixing X-vs-Y with
a tech card can flip X-vs-Z. Budget 1–2 card+weight cycles here.

## Tests

- Final tournament run at the 12.0 baseline seed is the acceptance
  test. The matchup matrix PDF (or the GUI's `Download results
  JSON`) is checked in as the closing artifact.
- CI gains one additional bench invocation guarding the matrix
  band — see [Step 10.2i](10.2-long-term-ai-plan.md#step-10.2i)
  for the harness.

## Commit template

Per tech-slot edit:

```
Step 12.6: deck — hollow-disruption tech slot vs ember-aggro.

-2 Whisperthief, +2 SilentFrost (1-Aether Unit, Cleanse). Shifts
the polar EmbHell-vs-HolReap cell from 22% to 44%.
```

Final closing commit:

```
Step 12.6: matchup matrix locked inside [25%, 75%].

Final tournament results at ai-testing-data/12.6-final.results.json.
Closes the step-12 balance arc.
```

## Exit criteria

- Every decisive-game cross-matchup cell in the final tournament
  matrix sits in `[25%, 75%]`.
- No pair dominates globally — top and bottom of the leaderboard
  differ by ≤ 25 pp.
- A final summary appended to `docs/plan/balance/` with the
  closing matrix, a before/after comparison against 12.0 baseline,
  and a short "what the bench tells us now" paragraph.
- All `stable/` profile promotions from 12.5 either survived or
  have a documented revert.

## Size

~1 day, with the caveat that cascading polarities may extend by
half a day per cycle. Budget 1.5 days to be safe.
