## Step 12.1 — AI floor fix

Eliminate AI misweighting as a confounder before touching anything
structural. Until the bots actually play toward the sole win
condition (Conduit collapse), you cannot tell whether a deck is
weak or the bot piloting it refuses to attack.

This step is also the **ongoing iteration point** of the step-12
arc. Every subsequent step ends with a mini-replay of this
procedure for the AIs whose scoring environment shifted.

Read first:
- [10.2-long-term-ai-plan.md](10.2-long-term-ai-plan.md) §"Authoring
  model — not raw knobs" and §"Production-ready vs WIP sets".
- The weights files under `encoding/ai/experimental/` that the
  12.0 baseline flagged as problematic.

## Scope — in

- Audit every `encoding/ai/experimental/*/weights.json`. Enforce the
  floor rule under the `pushing` and `lethal_check` intents:
  **`conduit_softness` ≥ `threat_avoidance`**. A bot that won't
  close when ahead isn't defensive — it's broken.
- Keep archetype identity in `default` and `early_tempo`: a Fortress
  bot is allowed to defend early, it just has to finish when ahead.
  Express the distinction per-intent, not by flattening the whole
  table.
- For each edited bot, run a mirror tournament against the pre-edit
  weights using the GUI's round-robin mode. Use the matched deck
  for each bot's archetype. Promotion bar: **≥ 55% decisive-game
  win rate over 40 games**.
- Promote passing bots to `encoding/ai/stable/` via `make
  ai-promote`; leave the rest `experimental/` with a note in their
  `notes.md` describing what went wrong.
- Write the edit summary to
  `docs/plan/balance/ai-floor-2026-04-20.md`: which bot was tuned,
  the bench delta, whether it promoted.

## Scope — out

- Authoring new considerations or BT nodes. Only the existing weight
  tables are in scope.
- Re-authoring the phase-BT (that's a separate arc if it's needed).
- Changing the `stable/current` pointer — that's a human decision
  once a stable candidate exists.

## Iteration point (applies to every later step in this arc)

Reweighting is the cheap-to-redo step that protects expensive-to-redo
deck and card work from regressing. **After every step 12.2–12.6
change, replay 12.1's procedure** on the bots whose scoring
environment changed. Mechanically: each PR that touches
`encoding/cards/*` or `encoding/decks/*` must include:

1. A fresh bench run using the matched AI as CPU.
2. A commit that re-promotes or re-pins affected AI profiles.

If the bench shows any matchup moved > 10 pp from the step-12.0
baseline, revisit 12.5 before merging.

## Tests

- Mirror benchmarks are the acceptance test. No xunit additions.

## Commit template

```
Step 12.1: AI floor fix — conduit_softness ≥ threat_avoidance under
pushing + lethal_check intents.

Tuned N bots, promoted M to stable/. Bench artifacts at
ai-testing-data/12.1-<bot>.results.json.
```

## Exit criteria

- Every experimental AI either satisfies the floor rule or carries
  a `notes.md` explaining why it was left as-is.
- Re-running `PairCorrectly.tournament.json` at the same seed as
  the 12.0 baseline shows average draw rate **≤ 40%**. If that
  target misses, the diagnosis shifts to 12.2 or 12.3 — log which
  and proceed.
- Promoted bots committed under `encoding/ai/stable/`.

## Size

~1 day for the initial pass. Recurring iteration cost thereafter:
0.5 day per deck/card change batch.
