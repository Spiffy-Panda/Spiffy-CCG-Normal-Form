### Why the draw rate moved

- **Knob 3** (harness integrity-delta tiebreaker; commit
  [`6f56a5b`](../../../src/Ccgnf.Bots/Bench/BotMatchRunner.cs))
  reclassifies cap-hit games where Conduits are standing but unevenly
  damaged. **No game-semantic change** — `conduit_softness`, Fortify,
  Mend, and every card still mean exactly what they did. Only the
  harness outcome-report changed.
- **12.3 + 12.4 before the knob** bought −22.5 pp of the drop from
  cards and decks themselves (81.7 → 59.2). The knob converted the
  remaining cap-hits with signal into decisive results.

### How to read this bench going forward

- **Draws = true stall.** Before Knob 3, most "draws" were harness
  cap-hits with real integrity deltas. Now every draw in the JSON
  is a genuine 0-delta stall cell — i.e., the game mechanically
  cannot resolve within the input budget for that matchup.
- **Decisive WR is load-bearing.** Raw WR hides stall cells; decisive
  WR reflects "how the matchup plays when it actually plays." Use it
  when comparing bots.
- **A pair's aggregate WR summed across matchups can exceed 50 %**
  without imbalance: the cross-pair format means if pair A beats
  pair B, B records a loss — totals don't sum to a single pie.

## Follow-ups, grouped by priority

### Primary next step — [`12.5`](12.5-matched-ai-tune.md) — matched-AI tune

**Why it's next:** Knob 3 converted cap-hit games into decisive
games, and the decisive view shows TiThWave running the field at
95.8 %. This wasn't visible pre-knob because the draws hid it. Until
wavebreaker (and probably fortress, given SimpleSweep's 91.8 %
mirror-draw rate) are tuned against the current card pool, 12.6's
cross-matchup polish can't land — tech cards can't fix a 90-pp
spread.

**Concrete sub-tasks:**

1. **wavebreaker pull-back.** `on_curve` 3.0 / 3.5 and
   `tempo_per_aether` 3.0 across every intent are probably over-high
   now that TIDE has real closers. Target: TiThWave decisive WR
   down to 65-75 %. Mirror-tournament bench against the current
   wavebreaker as per 12.5 promotion gate.
2. **fortress re-pair or retune.** SimpleSweep says fortress ≈
   wavebreaker on bulwark-control (58.8 % vs 61.3 % decisive WR).
   Either fortress needs a tune that exploits bulwark-control's new
   closers, or the deck's `suggested_ai` needs to change to whatever
   profile actually pilots it best. Triangulate with a deck-vs-every-bot
   bench before deciding.
3. **reaper.** HolReap's 2.9 % decisive WR survives Knob 3 as a real
   signal — reaper can't break the matchup triangle. See "Optional"
   below: may need a deck touch in 12.6 rather than pure weight
   tuning.
4. **Promote survivors to `encoding/ai/stable/`.** 12.1 never
   promoted anyone (all bots still live in `experimental/`). 12.5's
   promotion gate is the one chance to land the step-12 AIs in
   `stable/`.

### Secondary — [`12.6`](12.6-cross-matchup-polish.md) — cross-matchup polish

**Blocked by 12.5.** Open only once matched pairs are inside a
tighter spread. Work expected:

- **Tech-slot cards** for the two persistent stall cells:
  - *BulFort vs EmbHell* (37 draws): EMBER needs a *Sentinel-breaker*
    — a damage line that bypasses Fortification. This is a single
    uncommon EMBER card. Goes through the 12.3 card-cluster skill's
    gap-aware mode; card-pool audit gets regenerated once it lands.
  - *EmbHell vs HolReap* (30 draws): EMBER needs a threat that survives
    HOLLOW Phantom returns / removal, OR HOLLOW needs a way to force
    EMBER to deal with its board. Likely a tech slot on both sides.
- **Matrix re-verification** after each tech-slot edit. The 12.6 plan
  already has the cascading-polarity budget.
