Read both files. Before I write, a few design calls I want to flag so you can push back before I commit:

**The combinatorial hit is the biggest consequence.** Going from 5 → 7 factions takes you from 10 dual-faction signposts to 21. That's not a gentle scaling — it roughly doubles the design surface of every set, and draft packs have to be rebalanced (each faction is ~14% of the pool instead of ~20%, so two-color archetypes see fewer on-faction cards). Resonance thresholds need to come down across the board to keep draft playable.

**I'm keeping most of the chassis.** The five existing factions are already well-mapped to a classic color-pie; tearing them up to relabel would be vanity. I'll rename/retune them and add two genuinely new strategic poles that weren't served: **going wide** (tokens, swarm) and **modality** (choose-one cards). Those are the gaps in the current five.

**Keyword count stays flat.** I'll retire one, rename two for tone, and add two — net +/-0 from the original eleven, which matters for learnability.
