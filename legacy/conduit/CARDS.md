# Conduit — Example Cards

*A supplement to `RULES.md`. These cards illustrate faction identity, Resonance thresholds, Neutral utility, and dual-faction signposts. Numbers are first-pass playtest values, not finalized.*

Each entry uses the following shorthand:

> **Name** — Cost | Type (Subtype) | Faction | Rarity
> *Stats (Units only)*
> Keywords. Rules text.
> **Resonance [N]:** bonus.
> *flavor*

---

## Reading the designs

- **Commons** carry each faction's core identity at small cost. Their Resonance numbers are low (≤ 3) so that a two-color draft still lights them up reliably by the mid-game.
- **Uncommons** start to diverge into sub-archetypes and often scale Resonance up to [3–4].
- **Rares** and **mythics** push Resonance to [4–6], becoming full-throated rewards for mono-faction decks.
- **Neutrals** never carry Resonance, but provide structural glue — fixing, card filtering, utility removal.
- **Dual-faction signposts** (uncommon) never carry Resonance. Their entire design cost is "you must be in both factions to play this."

# Neutral — Structural glue

Neutral cards never have Resonance, and never contribute to any faction's Resonance totals. They exist to keep decks functional, reward splash plans, and smooth out draft pools.

### Drifter's Pack — 1 | Relic | Neutral | Common

*2 / 2*
*The factions pay in coin, like everyone else.*

### Hedge Archivist — 3 | Unit (Human Scholar) | Neutral | Uncommon

### Mercenary Captain — 4 | Unit (Human Warrior) | Neutral | Uncommon

# Illustrative Resonance curves

How a Pyre-heavy 40-card constructed deck experiences Resonance over a game:

| Turn | Typical Pyre cards in Discard | Which Resonance tiers are live |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0 | none |
| 2 | 1 (1-drop Tactic) | none |
| 3 | 2 | **[2]** live |
| 4 | 3–4 (combat trades) | **[2], [3]** live |
| 5 | 4–5 | **[2], [3], [4]** live |
| 6 | 5–7 | **[2], [3], [4], [5]** live |
| 7+ | 7+ | all tiers live, including the mythic [6] |

How a Pyre-splashed-in-Verdance deck (say, 8 Pyre cards in 40) experiences Resonance:

| Turn | Typical Pyre cards in Discard | Which Resonance tiers live |
|---|---|---|
| 1–4 | 0–1 | none reliably |
| 5 | 1–2 | **[2]** possibly |
| 6–7 | 2–3 | **[2]**, maybe **[3]** |
| 8+ | 3–4 | **[3]** reachable |

Notice the second deck can still play the splashed Pyre cards — they're functional — but Resonance is either late or never. That is the soft-faction pressure in action: the splash works, the *bonus* is the commitment tax.
